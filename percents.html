Fractions and Percents


Fractions


Percents are fractions, meaning they are parts of a whole.

For Example: if you ate 2 slices of pizza, you ate part of that whole pizza.

Now, depending on how many slices that whole pizza was cut into, determines the fraction, or percent, of that pizza you ate.

Let's say that pizza was cut evenly into 8 pieces.

Then, let's say you ate 2 of those pieces.

How much did you eat?

You ate: 2 out of 8 pieces.

In fraction form, this is written as: 2/8.

Percents


Learn more about percentages here: Percentages

The whole = eating all of the pizza.

Since it was cut into 8 total pieces of pizza, that means,
if you ate 8 of the slices, you ate ALL of the pizza.

In percent, that is written as: 100%.

However, since you only ate two of those pieces you ate 2/8 of it.

2/8 is one serving because 4 people are eating, so there are 4 servings.

Therefore, since you are one of 4 people eating, you ate 1/4 of the pizza.

In money terms: 1/4 = one quarter (or $.25 cents.)

Move the decimal 2 places to the right: .25 = 25.
Doing this gives you 25% of the pizza!

Here's another tip:
Think of percents as money! The whole represents $1.00.

That means the pizza, divided into 8 equal pieces, is = to $1.00.
If each person gets 2 pieces, you can feed 4 people.

Thinking of this as money, exchange people for coins.

Think: 4 quarters = $1.00. Each person eats $.25
or 1 quarter of the pizza. One quarter is written as: 25%!

  • Pennies: 100 pennies = $1.00, so 1 penny = 1%
  • Nickels: 20 nickels = $1.00, so 1 nickel = 5%
  • Dimes: 10 dimes = $1.00, so 1 dime = 10%
  • Quarters: 4 quarters = $1.00, so 1 quarter = 25%
